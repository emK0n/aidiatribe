About Us

Host & Creator

Jason Lowe

Consultant · Founder · Podcaster Aptivon · AI Diatribe · GTIA

Jason Lowe is the founder of Aptivon, an AI consultancy that moves organizations from curiosity to measurable ROI. Backed by 30 years in technology and hundreds of advisor and executive engagements, he blends deep technical fluency with an economist’s eye for impact. His vendor‑agnostic offerings - AI Readiness Assessments, Ideation Workshops, Strategic Road‑Maps, executive education, and vCAIO engagements - give companies a clear, actionable playbook for confident adoption.
Jason is an adjunct professor at the University of Utah's David Eccles School of Business and serves on the GTIA AI Advisory Council. A frank, energizing voice at AI and channel forums, he also hosts AI Diatribe, a video‑podcast delivering quick analyses and candid interviews on emerging tech. Audiences consistently call him enlightening, deeply knowledgeable, and a trusted ally in navigating the AI landscape.

Co-Host

Matt Konwiser

Field CTO · AI Ethicist · Columnist IBM · GTIA · AI Diatribe

Matt Konwiser is the co-host of AI Diatribe. His background has always been deeply rooted in techology working for large firms including Bay Networks/Nortel, Juniper, and currently IBM. Matt has been involved in the deployment of the high-speed commercial Internet, developing Enterprise architecture designs, and consulting for finance and healthcare organizations.
Matt uses his diverse background and training in marketing, technology and psychology to look at news, trends and tech in a different light and offer more human, relatable perspectives on what's happening and why. Matt is a storyteller by nature and often enjoys creative activities including photography, LEGO building, and gaming.

What is AI Diatribe?

AI Diatribe cuts through the noise of artificial intelligence hype to deliver sharp insights and bold takes on what really matters. Each episode features conversations with industry leaders from companies like IBM, ServiceNow, Meta, Walmart, and more.

From enterprise AI adoption and cybersecurity to the future of work and AGI timelines — no topic is off limits, and no punches are pulled.
